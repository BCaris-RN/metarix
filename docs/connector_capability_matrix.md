# Connector Capability Matrix

| Platform | Publish | Draft/Schedule | Analytics | Inbox/Comments | Listening | Notes/constraints |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Instagram | Yes | Yes | Yes | Yes | Partial | Business or creator-style access is typically required for richer publishing and insights flows. |
| Facebook | Yes | Yes | Yes | Yes | Partial | Page-based publishing and insights are practical for Meta Page workflows; app review and permissions are constrained. |
| LinkedIn | Yes | Yes | Yes | Limited | Limited | Posting and analytics are practical; comment and inbox workflows are more constrained than publish/report flows. |
| TikTok | Yes | Yes | Limited | Limited | Partial | Content Posting API supports direct publishing; research and moderation capabilities are more constrained. |
| YouTube | Yes | Limited | Yes | Yes | Limited | Upload and analytics are strong; comment moderation exists, but social-style inbox and broad listening are limited. |

## Source Notes

- Instagram and Facebook are based on Meta Graph and Instagram Graph developer documentation.
- LinkedIn is based on the LinkedIn Marketing API and posts/community management docs.
- TikTok is based on the Content Posting API, Research API, and developer guidelines.
- YouTube is based on the YouTube Data API and YouTube Analytics API documentation.
